# Service Pages Plan: High-Intent Local Keywords for Tender Gardens

## Overview

This plan focuses on Tender Gardens' two primary offers: ongoing native garden maintenance and handcrafted butterfly habitat installs. Each page template captures buyer intent while reinforcing Jaime’s Sarasota roots, solo delivery model, long-standing nursery relationships, and commitment to ongoing conservation education.

**Strategy:** Lead with maintenance-first keywords and butterfly garden intent phrases while reminding readers that Jaime personally delivers both services.

---

## Page 1: Native Garden Maintenance in [Primary City]

### Target Keyword
**Primary:** `native garden maintenance [city]` / `native plant maintenance service [city]`

**Secondary:**
- `monthly native garden care [city]`
- `Florida native plant maintenance`
- `native garden upkeep for snowbirds`

### Search Intent
Homeowners (often seasonal residents) with established native landscapes seeking a specialist who understands the difference between native volunteers and invasives and can provide consistent, low-input maintenance.

### URL Structure
`/services/native-garden-maintenance-[city-name]`

Example: `/services/native-garden-maintenance-venice-fl`

### Page Structure (750-900 words)

#### H1: Native Garden Maintenance Services in [City], Florida

#### Meta Title (60 chars):
`Native Garden Maintenance in [City], FL | Tender Gardens`

#### Meta Description (155 chars):
`Hands-on native plant garden maintenance in [City]. Monthly or bi-monthly visits, invasive control, and butterfly habitat care from owner Jaime Goulet.`

> **Keyword placement:** Use the exact phrase `native garden maintenance [City]` in the H1 and within the first 120 words. Include `native plant maintenance service [City]` once in the body copy and in at least one CTA.

#### Content Outline:

**Section 1: Introduction** (150 words)
- Local hook: “Serving [City] homeowners who want thriving Florida-native landscapes without the guesswork.”
- Mention that Jaime is a Sarasota native with deep familiarity with nearby habitats.
- Explain why native maintenance differs from standard landscaping (identifying volunteers vs. invasives, timing cutbacks to support wildlife).
- Reinforce personal service: every visit handled by Jaime within her four-day schedule.

**Section 2: What’s Included in Each Visit** (250 words)
- **Specialized Weeding:** Distinguish native seedlings from invasives (air potato, Caesar weed, tropical milkweed).
- **Seasonal Stewardship:** Timing for cutbacks, seed collecting, mulching, and storm prep.
- **Habitat Management:** Support butterfly and bird lifecycles; leave host plants when appropriate.
- **Plant Health Monitoring:** Catch pest or irrigation issues early; coordinate with trusted partners when outside scope.
- **Optional Add-Ons:** Mulch top-offs, native plant replacements sourced from preferred nurseries.
- Note Jaime’s long-standing relationships with trusted Sarasota native plant nurseries when discussing replacements.

**Section 3: Monthly vs. Bi-Monthly Programs** (175 words)
- Outline ideal cadence based on garden maturity.
- Highlight snowbird-friendly scheduling (extra visits before arrival/departure).
- Explain that availability is capped to protect 20-hour workweek; waitlist if schedule is full.

**Section 4: Why [City] Native Gardens Need a Specialist** (150 words)
- Local microclimates, coastal wind, and sandy soils.
- Invasive pressure unique to [City] neighborhoods.
- Importance of respecting wildlife windows (nesting seasons, butterfly broods).

**Section 5: Getting Started** (125 words)
- Brief discovery call → on-site maintenance assessment.
- Customized visit plan and estimate.
- Outline onboarding timeline (initial tidy-up followed by recurring cadence).
- CTA: “Schedule your native garden maintenance visit in [City].”
- Mention Jaime’s ongoing native plant education and how she shares seasonal insights during onboarding.

## Page 2: Butterfly Garden Installation in [Primary City]

### Target Keyword
**Primary:** `butterfly garden installation [city]`

**Secondary:**
- `monarch butterfly garden [city]`
- `pollinator garden installation [city]`
- `native host plant garden [city]`

### Search Intent
Homeowners commissioning small, high-impact butterfly habitats who want a native specialist to handle layout, sourcing, and install.

### URL Structure
`/services/butterfly-garden-installation-[city-name]`

Example: `/services/butterfly-garden-installation-venice-fl`

### Page Structure (850-1,000 words)

#### H1: Butterfly Garden Installation in [City], Florida

#### Meta Title (60 chars):
`Butterfly Garden Installation in [City] | Tender Gardens`

#### Meta Description (155 chars):
`Create a native butterfly habitat in [City]. Jaime installs small-scale gardens with host and nectar plants for monarchs, swallowtails, and more.`

> **Keyword placement:** Use `butterfly garden installation [City]` in the H1 and opening paragraph. Add `butterfly garden installation [County or nearby city]` in a subheading or CTA when relevant.

#### Content Outline:

**Section 1: Introduction** (150 words)
- Set expectation for handcrafted, small-scale installs (100-400 sq ft).
- Note that Sarasota-native Jaime brings long-standing habitat familiarity to each install.
- Mention common butterflies spotted in [City].
- Reinforce that Jaime leads every install and partners with other native experts only when a project exceeds her solo capacity.

**Section 2: Native Host Plants We Rely On** (200 words)
- Highlight plant lists for monarchs, swallowtails, fritillaries, longwings.
- Emphasize use of true Florida-native milkweed and avoidance of tropical milkweed.
- Note sourcing relationships with local nurseries.

**Section 3: Nectar Plants for Year-Round Bloom** (175 words)
- Break out seasonal bloomers (spring, summer, fall) to ensure succession.
- Mention structural grasses and shrubs for shelter.
- Include a sentence on Jaime’s ongoing native plant education and how new learnings influence nectar plant selections.

**Section 4: Installation Process** (225 words)
1. **Site Consultation:** Sun/shade mapping, soil assessment, client goals.
2. **Concept Sketch & Plant List:** Tailored layout with host/nectar balance.
3. **Installation Day:** Soil prep, planting, organic mulch, initial watering.
4. **Establishment Support:** Watering schedule, caterpillar protection tips, optional maintenance enrollment.

**Section 5: Collaboration Policy** (100 words)
- Explain criteria for inviting fellow native experts (projects larger than 400 sq ft or requiring hardscape support).
- Outline how Jaime remains the client point-of-contact even when collaborating, leaning on long-term nursery and expert relationships.
- Clarify limited availability to protect existing maintenance commitments.

**Section 6: Investment & Scheduling** (125 words)
- Typical range: $800-$2,200 depending on size and plant palette.
- Best planting seasons: fall and spring.
- Lead time expectations (2-4 weeks depending on nursery supply).
- CTA: “Request a butterfly garden install in [City].”
